MATHEMATICAL FORMALISM

A random-scale bridge on the sphere

The histogram looks approximately lognormal, but the geometry suggests a more informative construction. Each observed route is treated as a random transverse departure from the unique minor great-circle arc joining its endpoints.

1 / SPHERICAL PATH AND REFERENCE ARC

For aircraft \(i\), let the observed trajectory be \(x_i(t)\in S_R^2\), with normalized time \(0\leq t\leq1\). Its endpoint geodesic is

\[ g_i(s)=\operatorname{Exp}_{x_i(0)}\!\left(s\,\operatorname{Log}_{x_i(0)}x_i(1)\right), \qquad 0\leq s\leq1. \]

Here \(S_R^2\) is the Earth sphere and \(g_i\) is the minor great-circle arc from the first recorded position to the last.

2 / OBSERVED DEVIATION

The cross-track displacement is the shortest spherical distance from an observed position to the reference arc:

\[ \eta_i(t)=d_{S_R^2}\!\left(x_i(t),g_i([0,1])\right). \]

The primary histogram statistic is its discrete root-mean-square magnitude over the \(n=60\) observations:

\[ D_i=\left[\frac{1}{n}\sum_{j=1}^{n}\eta_i(t_j)^2\right]^{1/2}. \]

3 / LOGNORMAL HYPOTHESIS

The simplest positive, right-skewed population model assumes that many multiplicative route effects combine in logarithmic space:

\[ \log D_i\sim\mathcal N(\mu,\sigma^2), \] \[ f_D(d)=\frac{1}{d\sigma\sqrt{2\pi}} \exp\!\left[-\frac{(\log d-\mu)^2}{2\sigma^2}\right], \qquad d>0. \]

This is a useful empirical hypothesis, but by itself it does not explain why a route acquires its particular shape.

4 / RANDOM-SCALE BRIDGE

Separate the magnitude of a route's departure from its standardized shape:

\[ \eta_i(t)=A_i Z_i(t),\quad D_i=A_iQ_i,\quad Q_i=\left[\int_0^1 Z_i(t)^2\,dt\right]^{1/2}. \]

The bridge \(Z_i(0)=Z_i(1)=0\) represents endpoint-pinned route geometry, while \(A_i>0\) represents aircraft-specific scale. If \(\log A_i\) is Gaussian and variation in \(Q_i\) is comparatively modest, then \(\log D_i=\log A_i+\log Q_i\) is approximately Gaussian.

5 / BROWNIAN-BRIDGE BASELINE

A tractable baseline for the standardized route shape is a Brownian bridge with Karhunen-Loeve expansion

\[ Z(t)=\sum_{k=1}^{\infty}\frac{\sqrt{2}\,\xi_k}{\pi k} \sin(\pi kt), \qquad \xi_k\overset{\mathrm{iid}}{\sim}\mathcal N(0,1). \]

Its integrated squared displacement is a weighted chi-square variable:

\[ Q^2=\int_0^1Z(t)^2\,dt =\sum_{k=1}^{\infty}\frac{\xi_k^2}{\pi^2k^2}. \]

6 / RESULTING POPULATION LAW

If the scale \(A\) is lognormal and the bridge functional \(Q\) follows the generalized chi law above, their product has density

\[ f_D(d)=\int_0^{\infty} f_A\!\left(\frac{d}{q}\right)f_Q(q)\,\frac{dq}{q}. \]

This lognormal scale mixture is the geometry-driven analytical model. A single lognormal is recovered as an approximation when the bridge-shape factor \(Q\) is concentrated relative to the scale factor \(A\).

Interpretation. The lognormal appearance can arise from multiplicative variation in route scale, while systematic departures from lognormality encode differences in route shape, flight phase, airway structure, weather avoidance, and holding behavior. A heterogeneous population may therefore require a mixture \(f_D(d)=\sum_c\pi_c f_D(d\mid c)\) rather than one universal law.

ENERGY AND FUEL COUNTERFACTUAL

What would the excess ground track cost?

For the quality-filtered cohort, sum the observed polyline excess over the endpoint geodesic, \(\Delta s_\Sigma=\sum_i(L_i-G_i)\). In steady, level flight, lift approximately balances weight and aerodynamic work is drag times distance:

\[ \mathcal D\approx\frac{L}{L/\mathcal D}\approx\frac{Mg}{L/\mathcal D}, \qquad \Delta E_{\mathrm{mech}}\approx\frac{Mg}{L/\mathcal D}\,\Delta s_\Sigma. \]

With overall fuel-to-propulsive efficiency \(\eta\), jet-fuel net calorific value \(H_f\), density \(\rho_f\), U.S. gallon volume \(V_{\mathrm{gal}}\), and price \(p_f\),

\[ \Delta m_f=\frac{\Delta E_{\mathrm{mech}}}{\eta H_f}, \qquad \Delta C=\frac{\Delta m_f}{\rho_fV_{\mathrm{gal}}}\,p_f. \]
